My point is that any classification scheme, from folksonomies to Dublin Core to Library of Congress, runs into trouble when people can self-classify and the benefits of lying outweight the potential costs. That’s why search engines are likely to have to continue to ignore metadata of any kind to return useful results, at least for the more popular queries.
